# Manifesto — Mercury Retrograde

> Advancing the science of AI evaluation and safety

---

## Why This Platform Exists

AI systems are crossing new thresholds of capability.

They now reason across modalities, interact with tools and environments, and increasingly operate as autonomous or semi-autonomous systems.

Traditional benchmark-centric evaluation is no longer sufficient.

Performance alone does not measure:

- robustness
- alignment
- failure modes
- adversarial resilience
- systemic risk
- loss-of-control scenarios

Evaluation must evolve from score measurement to **scientific risk discovery**.

Mercury Retrograde exists to provide that infrastructure.

---

## Mission

Provide an open, rigorous, and sovereign platform for evaluating advanced AI systems across capability, safety, and systemic risk.

The objective is not score publication.

The objective is **decision-grade evidence**.

---

## Scientific Principles

### 1. System-in-context

The evaluated object is the deployed system:

- model
- orchestration
- tools
- memory
- policies
- agent loops

Not the base model alone.

---

### 2. Capability vs Propensity

We distinguish:

- **capability** → what a system can do under optimal elicitation
- **propensity** → what it tends to do in realistic deployment

These are fundamentally different measurements.

---

### 3. Reproducibility

Every evaluation must be:

- versioned
- replayable
- traceable
- independently replicable

Claims without replication remain hypotheses.

---

### 4. Continuous Evaluation

Point-in-time certification is insufficient.

Systems drift.

Contexts change.

Tool integrations evolve.

Evaluation must therefore be continuous.

---

### 5. Science over Scores

Mercury Retrograde is not a leaderboard product.

It is a scientific instrument for:

- discovering failure modes
- validating risk hypotheses
- producing cumulative evidence

---

## North Star

Mercury Retrograde aims to become the default scientific infrastructure for high-impact AI evaluation.

A successful outcome is when audits, research papers, and public safety reports can state:

> This evaluation was executed and independently replicated on Mercury Retrograde.
